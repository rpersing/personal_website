Digital Methodology

For my Digital Methodology project, I've chosen to do the Text Analysis module. I feel it neccessary to add that I am torn between the Text Analysis module and the Networks module. Both are topics that I am interested in researching, and in many ways they can overlap. Another struggle I had was coming up with an analysis question for this project and ultimately an idea for Text Analysis came to mind before an idea for networks did.

With that said, what I will be analyzing with text anaylsis is how music from the early 2000s is different than music from the 2010s. What I'm going to be looking at specifically is what the overall mood of the music is. For example, was music from the 2000s more sappy than music from the 2010s? For my data, I will be using the top 10 songs from the 2000s and the top 10 songs from the 2010s.

In order to determine "mood", I will be using a sentiment analysis tool that I found while doing some research for this project. This is how the website explains how the tool works:

The tool computes a sentiment score that reflects the overall sentiment, tone, or emotional feeling of your input text. Sentiment scores range from -100 to +100, where -100 indicates a very negative or serious tone and +100 indicates a very positive or enthusiastic tone.

I've embedded the the Google Sheet that I used to keep track of all the sentiment scores so that you can view the results for yourself. A few of the sentiment scores surprised me, but that's what makes this stuff interesting. Overall, what I gather from this is that people like more positive and enthusiastic. We, of course, already knew that, but it's nice to see it quantified and reinforced.
